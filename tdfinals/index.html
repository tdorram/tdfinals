Worms

Letting your cat outside can be dangerous, no matter how long it begs to go out there. Your first thoughts may be larger predators, humans, or treacherous adventures. But, there are smaller beings that can harm your feline! Worms are aquired by cats when they swallow fleas or feces during a grooming session, eating mice or other small prey that are infected, or even getting bitten by an infected mosquitoe! The easiest way to prevent this from happening with your pet is to keep them indoors. But, for our more adventurous cats who can't live without the thrill of the outdoors, there are some more steps that can be taken in order to keep these pets away from your fur babies.

Ways to Prevent Worms:

  • Regular Deworming: Use a deworming treatment on your cat regularly to prevent and kill any existing worms that they may have aquired. The treatments usually come in tablets, but can also be injections or skin treatments.
  • Stool Testing: Test your cat's stool at least twice a year if they are indoor cats, and four times a year if they are outdoor cats.
  • Keep litter box clean: Keeping your cat's bathroom clean is a very simple step to making sure your cat does not attain worms. If they step in infected feces and groom it off of themselves, it can easily infect them.
  • Change food and water bowls: Changing their food and water bowls regularly and even every single day is a simple and effective way to prevent worms.

Preventing Anxiety

Cats can easily get anxiety and if you don't see the signs, symtoms can get worse and lead to the need of medical attention. Some things that can cause stress in cats are environmental changes, owning other pets, loud noises, separation, being handled too much, and being kept in a small space for too long. In order to make sure your cat doesn't get stressed about these things, it is best to make sure they are slowly welcomed to their new home and fellow pets, as well as given a good amount of attention and space to roam. Even having cat beds, houses, or even hidden spaces for them to go when they feel they need to be left alone, cat greatly benifit you and your cat!
